About Us

When was the Porsche 911 first launched?

The first ever Porsche 911 was designed by F.A. Porsche – son of the founder of the company, Ferry Porsche – and his team.

The original 911 had an air-cooled flat-six engine instead of the four-cylinder boxer engine of the 356.

It developed 130PS, could accelerate from 0-100km/h in 9.1 seconds and had a top speed of 210km/h.

Where is the Porsche 911 built?

The Porsche 911 is only built at Zuffenhausen, Germany, the headquarters of Dr. Ing. h.c. F. Porsche AG.

It’s exactly the same factory complex, in fact, where the first-ever 911 rolled off the line back when full-scale production began in 1964, a year after its Frankfurt International Motor Show launch.

Every new 911 that has ever been built since has been made at Zuffenhausen.

How many Porsche 911 cars have been built?

In May 2017, the one-millionth Porsche 911, a Carrera S (type 991) in Irish Green, rolled off the Zuffenhausen production line.

Featuring nods to the original 1963 model, it included Pepita seat inserts, vintage Porsche crests, and green font dials.

After a world tour, it found a permanent home at the Porsche Museum in Stuttgart.

Who designed the Porsche 911?

The first Porsche 911, designed by F.A. Porsche and his team, marked a significant evolution from its predecessor, the 356. It featured an air-cooled flat-six engine delivering 130PS, a 0-100 km/h time of 9.1 seconds, and a top speed of 210 km/h—impressive performance for its time.

While the 911 has undergone many updates, it has retained signature elements like its 2+2 seating layout and rear engine position.

Over the decades, iconic designers have played a pivotal role in the 911's development. Anatole Lapine crafted the G series, the successor to the original 911, while Harm Lagaay, Porsche's chief designer from 1989 to 2004, introduced the distinctive "fried egg" headlights on the 996 model.
